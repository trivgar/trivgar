What is Claude Code?

Claude Code is an AI assistant that works directly with your files and folders. It can read documents, help you write and edit, organize research, and much more.

  • Read and understand your documents
  • Help draft, edit, and refine writing
  • Organize and summarize research materials
  • Create outlines, syllabi, and course materials
  • Analyze data and generate reports
  • Answer questions about your files

It uses your Claude Pro subscription ($20/month) ‚Äî no separate API costs. When you first run it, it opens a browser window to sign into your Claude account.

  1. Open File Explorer (press Win + E)
  2. Navigate to the folder containing your project or documents

The first time, it will open a browser window to sign into your Claude account. After that, it just starts up directly.

What is Tailscale?

üîó The Simple Explanation

Tailscale is like a secret tunnel between your devices. It connects your phone, laptop, and Raspberry Pi together in a private network ‚Äî even when they're in completely different locations.

Normally, your home devices are hidden behind your router and you can't reach them from outside. Tailscale solves this by creating secure connections between your devices, no matter where they are.

üåê Why This Matters for Your Pi-hole

Right now, your Pi-hole only blocks ads when you're at home. But with Tailscale, you can route your phone's internet traffic through your Pi ‚Äî even when you're miles away. This means:

  • ‚úÖ Ad-blocking on your phone at coffee shops, airports, work
  • ‚úÖ Access Pi-hole dashboard from anywhere
  • ‚úÖ SSH into your Pi remotely for maintenance
  • ‚úÖ All traffic encrypted and private

üîí How It Works

Your Phone (anywhere) ‚Üê‚îÄ‚îÄüîê‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí Tailscale Network ‚Üê‚îÄ‚îÄüîê‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí Your Pi (at home)

When you enable your Pi as an "exit node," all your phone/laptop traffic goes through your Pi first. Since Pi-hole is running there, it blocks ads before they ever reach your device.

üí∞ Cost

Free for personal use. Tailscale's free tier includes up to 100 devices, which is way more than you'll ever need.

Prerequisites Checklist

  • Raspberry Pi 5 with Pi-hole installed and running
  • Pi connected to your network (ethernet recommended)
  • SSH access to your Pi working
  • Your Pi's IP address (check Fing app or router)

Part 1: Connect to Your Pi

Step 1.1: SSH into your Pi

From Git Bash on your PC, connect to the Pi:

ssh pihole@YOUR_PI_IP

Replace YOUR_PI_IP with your Pi's actual IP address (e.g., 192.168.1.55)

Default password: raspberry

Expected output: You should see a command prompt like pihole@pihole:~$

Part 2: Install Tailscale on Your Pi

Step 2.1: Install Tailscale

Run this command on your Pi:

curl -fsSL https://tailscale.com/install.sh | sh

Expected output: Installation messages ending with "Installation complete"

Step 2.2: Start Tailscale as Exit Node

This lets you route all traffic through your Pi:

sudo tailscale up --advertise-exit-node

Expected output: A URL to authenticate. Open it in your browser.

Step 2.3: Authenticate

  1. Copy the URL from the terminal
  2. Open it in your browser
  3. Sign in with Google (or create a Tailscale account)
  4. Authorize the device

Expected result: Terminal shows "Success" and your Pi is connected.

Step 2.4: Approve Exit Node in Admin Console

  1. Go to Tailscale Admin Console
  2. Find your Pi in the machines list
  3. Click the ‚ãÆ menu ‚Üí Edit route settings
  4. Enable "Use as exit node"
  5. Save

Step 2.5: Get Your Pi's Tailscale IP

tailscale ip -4

Expected output: An IP like 100.x.x.x ‚Äî write this down!

Success! Your Pi is now on your Tailscale network and can act as an exit node.

Part 3: Install Tailscale on Your Other Devices

On Your Phone (iPhone or Android)

  1. Download the Tailscale app from App Store or Play Store
  2. Sign in with the same Google account you used for your Pi
  3. Tap the ‚ãÆ menu ‚Üí Use exit node
  4. Select your Pi from the list

Now all your phone's traffic routes through your Pi ‚Äî ads blocked everywhere!

On Your Laptop (Windows)

  1. Download Tailscale from tailscale.com/download/windows
  2. Install and sign in with your Google account
  3. Click the Tailscale icon in your system tray
  4. Select Exit node ‚Üí choose your Pi

Part 4: Test It!

Verify Everything Works

  1. Make sure your phone/laptop is using the Pi as exit node
  2. Visit http://YOUR_TAILSCALE_IP/admin in a browser
  3. You should see your Pi-hole dashboard!
  4. Visit an ad-heavy website ‚Äî ads should be blocked
  5. Check Pi-hole dashboard ‚Äî you should see queries coming in
Pro tip: Turn off the exit node when you need maximum speed (like video streaming). Turn it back on when browsing normally.

Troubleshooting

Can't connect to Pi via Tailscale

# On the Pi, check Tailscale status
tailscale status

# Make sure it's running
sudo systemctl status tailscaled

Exit node not working

  • Make sure you approved it in the Tailscale admin console
  • Try disconnecting and reconnecting on your phone/laptop
  • Check that your Pi has internet access

Slow internet when using exit node

All traffic goes through your home internet, so speed depends on your home upload speed. This is normal ‚Äî disable exit node for bandwidth-heavy tasks like streaming.
